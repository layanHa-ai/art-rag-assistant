Impressionism was an art movement that started in France during the 19th century.

Claude Monet was one of the founders of Impressionism.

Impressionist artists focused on light, color, and everyday scenes.

Surrealism explored dreams, imagination, and the unconscious mind.

Salvador Dalí was one of the most famous Surrealist artists.

Cubism was developed by Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque.

Cubism used geometric shapes and multiple perspectives.

Abstract art does not try to represent reality directly.

Vincent van Gogh was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter.

The Starry Night is one of van Gogh's most famous paintings.

The Louvre Museum in Paris is one of the most famous museums in the world.

Mona Lisa is displayed in the Louvre Museum.

Oil painting is one of the most common techniques in classical art.

Watercolor painting uses pigments mixed with water.

Digital art became popular with the development of computers and graphic tablets.

Islamic art is known for geometric patterns, calligraphy, and arabesque designs.

Pop Art became popular in the 1950s and used imagery from popular culture.

Andy Warhol was a major Pop Art artist.

The Persistence of Memory is a famous painting by Salvador Dalí.

Picasso painted Guernica to express the horrors of war.
