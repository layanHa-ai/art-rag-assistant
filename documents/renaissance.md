# Renaissance Art

The Renaissance was a major cultural movement that began in Italy during the 14th century and spread across Europe.

The word Renaissance means "rebirth" and refers to the renewed interest in classical Greek and Roman culture, science, philosophy, and art.

Renaissance art focused on realism, perspective, emotion, balance, and accurate human anatomy.

Artists during the Renaissance studied mathematics and science to improve their artistic techniques.

Florence was considered the center of Renaissance culture and artistic innovation.

Important Renaissance artists included Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael, and Donatello.

## Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci was an Italian painter, inventor, engineer, and scientist.

He is considered one of the greatest artists in history.

Leonardo painted the Mona Lisa, which is famous for the subject's mysterious smile.

He also painted The Last Supper, one of the most studied religious paintings in the world.

Leonardo used techniques such as sfumato to create soft transitions between colors and shadows.

He filled notebooks with drawings of machines, anatomy, and scientific observations.

## Michelangelo

Michelangelo was an Italian sculptor, painter, and architect.

He created the sculpture of David, which became a symbol of Renaissance humanism.

Michelangelo painted the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel in Vatican City.

The Sistine Chapel ceiling contains scenes from the Bible, including The Creation of Adam.

Michelangelo believed sculpture was the highest form of art.

## Raphael

Raphael was famous for harmony, balance, and clarity in his paintings.

One of his most famous works is The School of Athens.

The School of Athens depicts famous philosophers such as Plato and Aristotle.

Raphael's paintings were admired for their calm compositions and graceful figures.

## Renaissance Techniques

Linear perspective was an important technique developed during the Renaissance.

Perspective allowed artists to create depth and realistic space in paintings.

Chiaroscuro used strong contrasts between light and dark to create volume.

Artists carefully studied human anatomy to make figures appear realistic.

Oil painting became more advanced during the Renaissance period.

## Renaissance Architecture

Renaissance architecture was inspired by ancient Roman buildings.

Architects used columns, domes, arches, and symmetry.

Filippo Brunelleschi designed the dome of Florence Cathedral.

Buildings emphasized proportion and geometric harmony.

## Renaissance Sculpture

Renaissance sculptures often portrayed realistic human bodies and emotions.

Marble and bronze were commonly used materials.

Donatello was one of the first sculptors to revive classical techniques.

## Influence of the Renaissance

The Renaissance influenced art, literature, science, education, and philosophy.

Humanism became an important intellectual movement during this period.

Artists gained higher social status and were respected as intellectuals.

The Renaissance helped shape modern Western art and culture.

## Famous Renaissance Paintings

- Mona Lisa by Leonardo da Vinci
- The Last Supper by Leonardo da Vinci
- The Creation of Adam by Michelangelo
- The School of Athens by Raphael
- Birth of Venus by Sandro Botticelli

## Characteristics of Renaissance Art

- Realistic human anatomy
- Perspective and depth
- Balance and harmony
- Classical Greek and Roman influence
- Detailed facial expressions
- Natural lighting and shadows
- Emotional realism
